    What Personal Information is Collected

    Personal Information includes, but is not limited to, usage data, along with the name, telephone number, address, and e-mail address. Densho collects information from our users at several different points on our website:

    Densho eNews Email Newsletter

    The Densho eNews email newsletter is an optional monthly bulletin sent to people interested in the Densho project and related activities. Individuals must request to subscribe (i.e., "opt-in") at the Densho web site or through personal contact. Subscribers may choose to unsubscribe at any time and unsubscribe information is located at the bottom of each eNewsletter. Densho uses the Groundspring.org EmailNow service to send and manage the email list.

    Cookies, Tracking Codes and Web Traffic Analysis

    A cookie is a small packet of information that Densho puts on your computer's hard drive whenever a user visits our website. Tracking codes are small pieces of computer code that are passed to your web browser when it requests pages from our website. For each visitor to our website, this allows our servers to automatically recognize the following:

    Your IP address which may imply your geographic location
    How you were referred to the website
    The pages you have viewed, how long you spent on the website and when you visited
    Browser type and capabilities (e.g., "Firefox")
    Platform dependant information (e.g., "MacOS 10.7")

    Densho also uses Google Analytics to analyze traffic to this website. This analytic service does not create individual profiles for visitors; it collects only aggregate data. For information about Google Analytics, click here.

    In addition, Densho may examine and analyze technical logs created by the servers that operate the website. These logs may contain data similar to that described above.

    How the Information is Used

    Cookies and tracking codes are used to monitor the traffic on our website so we can determine how content is used and navigated, improve the user experience, and address technical issues with service capacity and availability.

    Comments are used to revise, correct, update, and evaluate the content of our website.
